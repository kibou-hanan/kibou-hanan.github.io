HCP: 29% of retailers expect an increase in sales for Q4-2021

29% of retailers expect an increase in the overall volume of sales for the fourth quarter of 2021, compared to 12% expecting a decrease, according to the High Commission for Planning (HCP). In its recent note on the results of its quarterly business surveys in wholesale trade and non-financial market services, HCP said this change is mainly explained by the expected increase in sales in “Wholesale of food and drink,” and “Other specialized retailers.” Orders planned for Q4-2021 would be at a normal level according to 80% of business leaders, and employment would experience stability in the workforce according to 91% of retailers. In the third quarter of this current year, sales of the Wholesale trade sector on the local market remained stable according to 58% of retailers and increased according to 25% of retailers, says the same source. This change is mainly due to the increase in sales in “Wholesale of food and drink products,” “Other specialized retailers,” and, on the other hand, a drop in sales was noticed in “Wholesale of information and communication equipment.” During the third quarter of this year, employment remained stable according to 71% of business leaders, HCP said, adding that 80% of retailers reported normal stock levels. The observed trend in selling prices was stable, according to 64% of business leaders, and increased according to 30%.

Morocco's Official Reserve Assets Up 5.2%

Morocco’s official reserve assets amounted to 330.5 billion dirhams on December 17, 2021, up by 5.2% compared to the same period in 2020, Morocco’s central bank (Bank Al-Maghrib) has said. During the week of December 16-22, 2021, Bank Al-Maghrib injected 83.3 billion dirhams. As for the interbank rate, it has remained at 1.5%, the source added in a release. In the same period, the Dirham remained almost stable against the U.S. Dollar and depreciated by 0.20% against the Euro.
