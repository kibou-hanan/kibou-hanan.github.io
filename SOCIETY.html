5 hotels in Agadir to benefit from state financed renovations

Five agreements relating to the program of granting subsidies dedicated to the renovation and upgrading of hotels in Agadir were signed this Tuesday, December 29. These five establishments represent the first batch of beneficiaries of the governmental program in the tourist seaside area, the city center and the Talborjt district, namely, Atlantic Palace, Sofitel Thalassa, Amadil, Aferni and Amenou, according to a statement from the Wilaya of the Souss-Massa region. The overall cost of renovation and project upgrade is estimated at MAD 300 million, while additional subsidies granted by the program amount to MAD 32.5 million.

Morocco’s electricity productions saw a 6.2% increase in October

orocco’s Directorate of Studies and Financial Forecasts (DEPF) reported an increase of 6.2% in electrical energy production in the country during the first 10 months of 2020, up from 4.5% a year earlier. DEPF attributed this positive trend to high private electricity production (5.5%), as well as a performance by the Moroccan Office of Electricity and Drinking Water (ONEE) (10.1%). A 3.9% increase in electrical energy production was also attributed to renewable energies, according to DEPF. According to the DEPF’s December economic report, Morocco’s energy production has increased by 1.4% compared to its pre-COVID crisis level. At the end of October, electricity consumption increased by 5.8% following an increase of 10.1% a month earlier and a decline of 2.8% during the previous year. The statistics cover an increase in sales of “Very High, High, and Medium Voltage” energy.
